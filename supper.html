Yam Porridge (Asaro)

Yam porridge, also known as Asaro, is a popular Nigerian dish made with yam, tomatoes, peppers, and various seasonings. It is a hearty and flavorful meal that is often enjoyed as a main course for lunch or dinner. The dish is known for its rich taste and comforting texture, making it a favorite among many Nigerians.

Ingredients
Quantity Ingredient Instruction
1 medium tuber White yam
¼ cup Palm oil
2 medium Habanero pepper
1 medium/large Scotch bonnet Optional
4 medium Fresh tomatoes
1½ medium Onion
2 tbsp Crayfish Ground
3 Stock cubes Crushed
To taste Salt

Instructions

  1. Peel the yam and cut it into medium-sized cubes. Rinse the yam pieces under cold water to remove any excess starch and set aside.
  2. Blend the fresh tomatoes, habanero pepper, scotch bonnet (if using), and onions until smooth. Set the tomato mixture aside.
  3. Heat the palm oil in a large pot over medium heat until it becomes hot. Ensure the oil does not smoke.
  4. Add the blended tomato mixture to the pot and cook uncovered on medium heat until the sauce thickens and the oil begins to separate from the tomato mixture.
  5. Add the yam cubes to the pot and stir to coat them with the tomato sauce.
  6. Pour in enough water to cover the yam cubes. Stir to combine and bring to a boil.
  7. Reduce the heat to medium-low, cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid, and simmer for about 10-15 minutes or until the yam is soft and cooked through.
  8. Add the ground crayfish, crushed stock cubes, and salt to taste. Stir well to combine.
  9. Continue to cook for an additional 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent burning. If the porridge is too thick, you can add a little more water to reach your desired consistency.
  10. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. Remove from the heat and allow to rest for 2 - 3 minutes.
  11. Garnish with chopped fresh herbs if desired, and serve the yam porridge hot.
