Puff Puff

Puff Puff is a popular West African snack made from deep-fried dough. It's soft, fluffy, and slightly sweet, making it a delightful treat for any occasion. Perfect for parties, gatherings, or just a tasty snack to enjoy with friends and family. This recipe caters for 20 - 25 pieces of Puff Puff.

Ingredients
Quantity Ingredient Instruction
3 cups All-purpose flour Sift if lumpy
½ cup Sugar Adjust to taste
2¼ tsp(1 sachet) Instant yeast
1½ cups Warm water Not hot
¼ tsp Salt
¼ - ½ tsp Nutmeg Optional
For deep frying Vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Add the yeast, sugar and warm water into a bowl to activate the yeast. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. Add flour, salt, and nutmeg (if using) into the yeast mixture. Mix well to form a smooth batter. The batter should be thicker than pancake batter, but thinner than dough.
  3. Cover the bowl with cloth or plastic wrap and leave in a warm place for 45-60 mins. The batter should double and look bubbly.
  4. Heat the oil in a deep fryer or large pan over medium heat. Test with a drop of batter; it should rise slowly.
  5. Use a spoon or your hand to scoop small portions of the batter and gently drop them into the hot oil. Fry in batches, avoiding overcrowding.
  6. Fry the Puff Puff for about 3-5 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are golden brown on all sides.
  7. Use a slotted spoon to remove the Puff Puff from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  8. Serve warm. Enjoy your delicious homemade Puff Puff!
