Jollof Rice With Fried Chicken

A popular West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, and spices, often served with fried chicken or turkey, fried plantain or banana with a cold drink. This serving is for 4 – 6 people.

Ingredients
Quantity Ingredient Instruction
Fried Chicken
700g Chicken
½ medium Onion Sliced
2 cloves Garlic Grated
1 tsp Ginger Grated
1 tsp Nutmeg Ground
½ tsp Curry powder
½ tsp Thyme
1 Stock cube Crushed
To taste Salt
Jollof Rice
4 cups Long grain parboiled rice
5 medium Fresh tomatoes
2 large Red bell pepper (Tatashe)
2 medium/large Scotch bonnet Optional
3 medium Onions
2 sachets Tomato paste
1 bulb Garlic
1 tbsp Ginger Grated
1 tbsp Nutmeg Ground
2 tsp Thyme
2 tsp Curry powder
4 Stock cubes Crushed
2 Bay leaves
To taste Salt
½ cup Vegetable oil
2 sticks Unsalted butter

Instructions

Fried Chicken

  1. Cut into medium pieces and wash the chicken into a pot.
  2. Add the sliced onions, garlic, ginger, curry powder, thyme, stock cube, and salt.
  3. Add enough water to cover the chicken pieces and stir to combine.
  4. Cook until the chicken is tender, then remove the chicken and set aside the stock for use in the Jollof Rice.
  5. Heat oil in a deep pan and fry the chicken pieces until golden brown. Set aside.

Jollof Rice

  1. Add the rice into a bowl and pour in hot water until it covers the rice. Let it soak for about 20 minutes, drain the water, wash it, and set it aside.
  2. Blend the fresh tomatoes, red bell pepper, scotch bonnet, and 2 onions until smooth.
  3. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot and saut√© the remaining sliced onion until soft and fragrant.
  4. Add the tomato paste and fry for 5 - 7 minutes until the oil separates from the paste, stirring frequently to prevent burning.
  5. Add the blended tomato mixture to the pot and cook uncovered on medium heat until the sauce thickens.
  6. Add garlic, ginger, nutmeg, thyme, curry powder, crushed stock cubes, and bay leaves. Cook for another 5 minutes.
  7. Add the pre-washed rice to the pot and stir to coat the rice with the tomato sauce.
  8. Pour in the reserved chicken stock and enough water to cover the rice by about an inch. Stir to combine and bring to a boil.
  9. Reduce the heat to medium-low, cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid, and simmer for about 20 minutes.
  10. Add the sticks of butter, more sliced onions (optional), sliced tomatoes (optional) and more spices depending on the taste. Stir them in and cover the pot.
  11. Remove from heat and let it sit covered for an additional 10 minutes. Fluff the rice with a fork before serving.
  12. Serve the Jollof Rice hot with the fried chicken on the side.
